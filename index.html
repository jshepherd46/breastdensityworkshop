A Message from Our Leadership

Letter from the Co-Chairs

The Importance of IBDW and Hawaiʻi as Our Venue

The International Breast Density and Breast Cancer Risk Assessment Workshop is a biennial scientific meeting dedicated to advancing breast density research, imaging biomarkers, and breast cancer risk assessment models. This workshop brings together leading researchers, clinicians, and program leaders from top academic institutions, industry, and government agencies to share cutting-edge research and collaborate on improving breast cancer detection and prevention.

Breast cancer is the leading cause of cancer-related deaths for women in their 50s in the U.S., and the Pacific Islands face unique cancer disparities due to geographic isolation, healthcare access challenges, and distinct population genetics. Hosting the workshop in Hawaiʻi ensures that experts from across the country and the world engage with these critical issues firsthand and collaborate with researchers and healthcare providers addressing cancer disparities in Pacific populations.

By gathering in Hawaiʻi, we foster global collaboration and develop more accurate breast cancer risk models, no matter the region of the world. As a historical nexus between East and West, Hawaiʻi has long been a meeting place for people and ideas from across the globe. This spirit of connection extends to IBDW, where past meetings have led to international partnerships dedicated to advancing breast cancer research for all populations.

John Shepherd, PhD University of Hawaiʻi Cancer Center
Karla Kerlikowske, MD UC San Francisco School of Medicine
